# Demo Transcript — Pinecrest Dental Group

**Call Date:** March 14, 2026
**Attendees:** Dr. Rachel Nguyen (Owner), Marcus Cole (Office Manager), Teegan (Flowsor)
**Source:** Granola summary + manual notes

---

**Teegan:** So tell me what's going on day to day that made you reach out.

**Marcus:** Honestly it's the patient follow-up stuff that's killing us. We have about 1,400 active patients across two locations. After every appointment we're supposed to send a follow-up — treatment plan reminders, recall scheduling, post-op care instructions. Right now that's all manual. Our front desk staff copies and pastes from Word templates, personalizes each one, sends it through our patient portal. Takes maybe 15 minutes per patient.

**Dr. Nguyen:** And they're not getting done. That's the real issue. We did an audit last month and only 40% of patients who needed follow-ups actually got them within 48 hours. The rest either went out late or never went out at all. I have two hygienists who are frustrated because they're recommending treatment plans and then nothing happens after the patient walks out the door.

**Teegan:** What's the downstream impact of that?

**Dr. Nguyen:** Revenue. Straight up. Our case acceptance rate dropped from 68% to 51% over the last year. I think a huge part of that is we're not reinforcing the treatment plan while it's still fresh. By the time we follow up — if we follow up — the patient has already talked themselves out of it or gone somewhere else.

**Marcus:** We also lost two front desk staff in the last six months. The ones we have are stretched thin. Hiring is brutal right now, we posted on Indeed for three weeks and got maybe four qualified applicants.

**Teegan:** Walk me through the tools you're using right now.

**Marcus:** Dentrix for practice management and scheduling. We use Weave for phones and some texting but honestly we barely scratch the surface of it. The Word templates I mentioned for follow-ups. And then just Outlook for email. Dr. Nguyen uses a shared Google Drive for internal docs but that's more for the clinical side.

**Teegan:** Have you looked at any automation tools before?

**Marcus:** We tried Mailchimp about a year ago for patient newsletters. Spent two months setting it up, sent maybe three campaigns, then nobody maintained it. The problem wasn't the tool, it was that nobody on the team has time to build and manage these systems. That's kind of why we're talking to you instead of just buying another SaaS product.

**Dr. Nguyen:** I don't want another login. I want someone to build the thing, show us it works, and hand it to us running.

**Teegan:** That's exactly how we work. What does your budget look like for something like this?

**Dr. Nguyen:** I told Marcus before this call, I'd spend $3,000 to $5,000 to get this solved if it actually works. We're losing way more than that in dropped treatment plans every month. I did the math — even a 10% improvement in case acceptance is worth about $12,000 a month to us.

**Teegan:** And timeline — is there urgency here or is this more of a "when we get to it" thing?

**Marcus:** We want to move on this. We have a team meeting in two weeks where Dr. Nguyen is presenting a whole operational improvement plan to the staff. Having at least a demo or pilot of the follow-up system by then would be huge. Even just showing the team "this is coming" would help morale.

**Dr. Nguyen:** "We're not hiring a fifth person for the front desk. We're going to work smarter." That's literally what I want to say at that meeting.

**Teegan:** Who else would need to sign off on this besides you two?

**Dr. Nguyen:** It's my practice. I make the call. Marcus handles implementation day to day. If you can convince both of us it works, we're in.

**Teegan:** Last question — if we do this, what does success look like six months from now?

**Dr. Nguyen:** Every patient who walks out of here with a treatment plan gets a personalized follow-up within 24 hours. No exceptions. And I want to see case acceptance back above 60%. That's the number.

**Marcus:** And I want my front desk staff to stop dreading Mondays. That's my number.

- They don't know what's possible with automation yet. The proposal should show them a vision beyond just email — think SMS, portal integration, maybe even AI-generated personalized care summaries.
- Two locations = need to confirm if workflows differ or are standardized.
